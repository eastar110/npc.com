Short Bio

Minlan Yu is a Gordon McKay professor at Harvard School of Engineering and Applied Science. She received her B.A. in computer science and mathematics from Peking University in 2006 and her M.A. and PhD in computer science from Princeton University in 2008 and 2011. She has actively collaborated with companies such as Google, AT&T, Microsoft, Facebook, Intel, and Bell Labs. Her research interests include data networking, distributed systems, enterprise and data center networks, and software-defined networking. She received the ACM SIGCOMM doctoral dissertation award in 2011 and NSF CAREER award in 2015. She served as PC co-chair for NSDI, HotNets, and several other conferences and workshops.
